StarBox - FAQ




How does StarBox differ from UltraBox? StarBox aims to expand apon UltraBox New features include:
  • Higher quality chipwaves (Rounded, Triange and Sawtooth)
  • More unison options / presets. Thank you MidBox!
  • More flexability coming to some restrictions, such as higher max BPM (Click on the number next to the slider to type in anything above 500) and -8 and +8 Octave options.
  • New themes, and the ability to make custom themes.
What are some planned things I should expect to come to StarBox soon™? Things I want to add:
  • The ability to export all channels as their own file, this will be very helpful for a few things I do (such as, bringing tracks into an audio editing program for better mixing and whatnot, putting your tracks into an oscilloscope program for viewing each channel's waveform [check out my youtube for an example of what I am talking about] :3 and prolly a few other things.
  • More instrument presets
  • More note effects (ring modulation, phaser, reverse reverb )
  • More themes
if you want to help, Please reach out to me on discord or on github!
How do I add custom samples? Since StarBox doesn't have a web server of its own, you'll have to first upload your sample to a CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing) -friendly website.
  • The easiest to use are File Garden or Catbox.
Next, you have to copy the URL of the sound itself. The URL should end with the filetype, such as .wav or .mp3.

To import it into StarBox, open the Edit tab, scroll to the bottom, and click on "Add Custom Samples..."

Paste your url and press "Okay." If everything goes smoothly, StarBox will reload and your sample will be available in the preset list.
Does StarBox have an offline version? Kind of- It does not have any executables or anything like UltraBox, but you can download everything here and it should work without an internet connection. Custom samples will not work sadly. I plan on getting this working when v1.0 comes out.
Does StarBox claim ownership over songs created with it? StarBox does not claim ownership over songs created with it, so original songs belong to their authors.

Neither StarBox or its creator assume responsibility for any copyrighted material played on StarBox. No songs are ever received, recorded, or distributed by StarBox's servers. All song data is contained in the URL after the hash (#) mark, and StarBox running inside your browser converts that data into sound waves.
